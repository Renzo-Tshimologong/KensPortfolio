KENNETH CARSON

From 1961 to the debut of Superstar Ken in 1977 Ken had straight arms that didn't bend. His head could only turn left and right. Ken's hair was made of felt in his first year (known to collectors as the "flocked" hair Ken), but this was replaced with a plastic, molded hairstyle when the creators realized that the felt hair fell off when wet.[3] Superstar Ken featured a dimpled smile, a head that could swivel, bent arms, a more muscular physique, jewelry, and underwear permanently molded to his body. The woman who made the Ken doll made it to resemble her husband.

A.I KITTEN

The idea of computational intelligence may come from observing the behavior of creatures. ACO was presented by studying the behavior of ants, and PSO was presented by examining the movements of flocking gulls. Through inspecting the behavior of the cat, Cat Swarm Optimization (CSO) is proposed. The artificial structure can be viewed as the model for modeling the common behavior of cats. CSO somehow belongs to the swarm intelligence. PSO with a weighting factor [11] usually finds the optimal solution faster than the others [10], but the proposed CSO may give much better performance than PSO with a weighting factor. CLONE KEN

Cloning is a technique scientists use to make exact genetic copies of living things. Genes, cells, tissues, and even whole animals can all be cloned. Some clones already exist in nature. Single-celled organisms like bacteria make exact copies of themselves each time they reproduce. In humans, identical twins are similar to clones. They share almost the exact same genes. Identical twins are created when a fertilized egg splits in two. Scientists also make clones in the lab. They often clone genes in order to study and better understand them. To clone a gene, researchers take DNA from a living creature and insert it into a carrier like bacteria or yeast. Every time that carrier reproduces, a new copy of the gene is made.
